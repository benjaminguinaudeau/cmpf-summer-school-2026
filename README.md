# Summer School for Journalists — Session Skeleton

**Created:** 2026-06-12
**Status:** Planning

## Objective

Prepare a 1.5-hour session (11:30–13:00) for a summer school for journalists, scheduled on the first day, setting the broader context of the transformation of the information environment, shifting dynamics of news authority, and implications for journalism. Audience: mid-career practicing journalists, mostly from Europe, some from Africa and other regions; highly engaged, appreciate active involvement.

Deliverable: `skeleton.md` structuring the session in English, with discussion led through open questions to the audience. Builds on prior slides: https://benjaminguinaudeau.github.io/ahuntsic-2026/#/tiktok-et-d%C3%A9mocratie

Key takeaways to structure:

- Transition from subscription to recsys creates a huge power asymmetry between the platform and producers/users.
- Two attention streams on recsys platforms: niche users consuming mostly political content; everyday users exposed to political news only around focus events.
- The second stream centers on moderate/centrist news, so extreme content gets less exposure.
- General competition for attention among all producers: incentives favor format and attention-bait at the expense of news quality.
- Virality is a dynamic equilibrium with the platform: no magic recipe, trends are pushed by the platform, so producers must follow trends and are responsive to the platform's agenda-setting.
- Normative problem: acceleration of the news cycle hurts the deliberative quality of public discourse.
- TikTok's American dream is an illusion: a few users capture most of the attention.
- Solving the asymmetry requires data transparency and third-party auditing capacity for scholars; journalists currently have no way to aggregate individual experiences to report on actual user experience.
