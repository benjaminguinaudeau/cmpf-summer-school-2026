# Project: Summer School for Journalists — Session Skeleton

## What This Is

A 90-minute interactive session skeleton (skeleton.md, in English) on the social media ecosystem and TikTok dynamics for mid-career journalists, structured around eight key takeaways and led with open questions to the audience.

## Decisions

- Six-block arc: opening hook (news authority) → Block 1 power asymmetry → Block 2 two attention streams + centrist exposure → Block 3 attention market (bait incentives, virality equilibrium, 5%/93% concentration) → Block 4 news-cycle acceleration + transparency/DSA → closing discussion. Each block opens with open questions before any input.
- ~40 of 90 minutes reserved for structured discussion; closing discussion is never cut (contingency trims Block 2 evidence and the recap instead).
- Block 2 runs as a prediction exercise (guess political share / typical viewer / radicalization show-of-hands) because the centrist-exposure finding is counterintuitive.
- skeleton.md is plain English markdown; French teaching-slides rules do not apply. Slides come later, reusing ahuntsic-2026 material (editor-in-chief framing, format–algorithm feedback loop, McLuhan point, US-study figures) with English labels.
